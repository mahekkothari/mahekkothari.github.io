Involvments

Besides my heavy cs coursework , I have a huge interest in fintech. I often spend my free time creating data visualizations, queries, and dashboards on various topics. One of them being using digital asset market data to create insights on crypto markets and decentralized finance by using plain SQL. Sharing my visualizations with my peers in a user-friendly manner, both through my computer science society and on Medium, has been a significant part of my college experience thus far. Developing the fintech space to be an inclusive environment has been extremely important to me. Through medium I have been about to write on gender equity in the finance space, as well as through clubs like Girls Who Code I have been given the opportunity to bridge the gap of inclusivity in both the tech and finance community. Using both my interests in technical communities and within social inquiry is something I have been (and will continue) to actively work through.


Getting Personal

Like most people I have had my shared attempts to find a hobby in hopes to replace my growing screen time. From being an avid crocheter for roughly 30 minutes to my attempt to run everyday lasting approximately 3 days – it’s easy to say it took a while for me to find what I truly like. In contrast to my short attention span, I love to read in my spare time. While running was short-lived, I love hiking (even though I have terrible pollen allergies), volleyball, and going on walks. Music has also been a huge influence on me, from creating hyper-niche playlists to finding out my hidden love for EDM. Balancing my time I spend checked online and the time I spend appreciating life outside of the screen has been a goal I have been working towards in the past year!
